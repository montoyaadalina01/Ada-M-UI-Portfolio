Overview

A smarter way to reclaim your screen time

Nudge is a social media screen-time manager that helps users set daily limits and choose how they want to be reminded when they hit them, without feeling punished for it.

The core idea

Most screen-time apps treat users like children hard blocks, alarms, shame. Nudge takes a different approach: give people agency over how they're interrupted. You pick your "Nudge Style." You set your limit. The app and Buddy, the cactus mascot does the rest without judgment.

4

Nudge styles designed

Passcode lock, full block, greyscale, and take a break each respecting different personalities and goals.

6

Core app screens

Onboarding, dashboard, nudge history, insights, settings, and intervention states all prototyped end-to-end.

5

Step onboarding flow

A guided setup that lets users configure their apps, limit, and nudge style before ever seeing the dashboard.

The problem

Awareness alone doesn't change behavior

Existing tools like Screen Time on iOS tell you how much time you've spent, but offer blunt, one-size-fits-all solutions when you go over. Hard blocks feel punishing. Soft reminders get ignored. Neither respects that different users need different interventions.

The question we asked: what if the app adapted to your personality, instead of forcing you to adapt to it?

⏱️

People underestimate usage

Users consistently report that they spend less time on social media than they actually do. awareness is the first step.

🚫

Hard blocks breed workarounds

Existing apps with total blocks are commonly bypassed by deleting and reinstalling apps, or using browsers.

🎯

Agency increases compliance

Users who choose their intervention method are more likely to respect it than users who have one imposed on them.

🌵

Tone matters

The cactus mascot "Buddy" was designed to notice without judging "Not mad. Just noticed." shifts the emotional dynamic.

Onboarding

Getting started in 5 steps

Before the user ever sees a dashboard, Nudge walks them through a focused setup flow building investment in their own choices.

01
Time limit

Set your daily limit

A slider and segmented control lets users pick their total daily social media time from 15 minutes to 4+ hours. Framed as "How long on social apps today?" rather than a restriction.

02
Intervention

Choose your nudge style

Four distinct options Passcode Unlock, Full Block, Greyscale, Take a Break each described with a tone, not a warning. Users select what fits their personality.

03
Exceptions

Set app exceptions

Some apps should never be blocked messaging, maps, payments. Users whitelist what matters so Nudge never gets in the way of what's important.

04
Tracking

Select apps to track

Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, YouTube, Reddit, Snapchat, Discord, Pinterest users choose exactly which apps count toward their limit.

05
Accountability

Create a passcode

For Passcode Unlock style users, this step creates the code a trusted person holds adding a social accountability layer that makes bypassing require real effort.

✓
Done

Dashboard ready

With setup complete, the dashboard populates with the user's chosen limits and starts tracking immediately. Buddy is watching gently.

Nudge styles

Four ways to say enough

The defining design decision of Nudge rather than one intervention for everyone, users choose the approach that fits how they think about limits.

🔐

Passcode unlock

When time runs out, a passcode screen blocks the app. The code is held by a trusted person parent, partner, friend adding real social friction to override it.

Social accountability
⛔

Full block

A hard stop. The app is completely inaccessible once the limit is reached. A mascot screen explains why "You've reached today's limit" with an option to request an override.

Maximum friction
🌑

Greyscale

The screen loses all color, making it visually unappealing without locking you out entirely. Restores at midnight. A subtle environmental cue that doesn't feel punitive.

Ambient friction
⏸️

Take a break

A gentle timer 15, 30, or 60 minutes asks the user to step away and come back refreshed. Buddy congratulates them on starting their break. Low pressure, high agency.

Gentle reminder

Reflections

What we learned

Designing Nudge pushed us to think about behavior change, emotional design, and the ethics of digital wellness.

01

Choice is the intervention

The single most impactful design decision wasn't any one nudge style it was giving users the choice. Autonomy in setup creates commitment to the outcome.

02

Tone is a design material

The language "Not mad. Just noticed." changed how the whole app felt. A mascot that notices without judging required constant copy and illustration iteration to get right.

03

Friction needs calibration

Too much friction and users abandon the app. Too little and it has no effect. The four nudge styles let users find their own equilibrium reducing churn from frustrated users.
