Overview

More than a tracker a full wellness companion

Flowkeeper combines cycle tracking, health education, community connection, and gamified learning into one cohesive iOS experience.

The core idea

Most period apps stop at logging dates. Flowkeeper goes further it educates users about what's happening in their body at each phase, connects them with a supportive community, and makes learning about reproductive health genuinely fun through a word-search game built around cycle vocabulary.

4

Core navigation tabs

Cycle, Game, Community, and Settings each a complete feature area with its own sub-screens and interactions.

10+

Unique screens designed

From lock screen and onboarding through all four tabs, including full game state progressions and community flows.

9

Game interaction states

The Sync & Seek word search has 9 progressive states as users find each cycle-related word, with a congrats screen and leaderboard.

4

Cycle journey paths

Users can select their journey: Cycle tracking, Trying to conceive, Partner mode, or Health management.

The Problem

Period apps track. They rarely teach.

The most popular cycle trackers are data collectors. They tell you when your period is coming but leave users in the dark about why their body behaves the way it does, and feeling isolated in the experience.

Flowkeeper was designed around a different question: what if an app could make you genuinely understand your cycle, not just log it?

🔍

Tracking without context

Most apps record symptoms and dates but don't explain the hormonal phases driving them leaving users confused about their own bodies.

💬️

Isolation in the experience

Menstrual health is still stigmatized. Users lack a safe space to share experiences, ask questions, and feel understood by a community.

🎒

Education feels clinical

Health education resources are often dry, inaccessible, or buried. Flowkeeper embeds learning directly into a game so it's frictionless.

🎯

One size fits no one

People use cycle apps for very different reasons tracking, conception, managing conditions. Flowkeeper supports multiple journey types from the start.

Core Features

Four tabs. One complete experience.

Each tab of Flowkeeper was designed as a full feature area not an afterthought.

01

Cycle Tracking

A full calendar view with color-coded period, ovulation, and predicted days. Users log symptoms, moods, and flow daily. Phase-specific wellness tips appear based on where they are in their cycle. A streak flame rewards consistency.

Core tab · Daily use
02

Sync & Seek Game

A word-search puzzle built around reproductive health vocabulary Estrogen, Luteal, Follicular, Menstrual, Ovulation. Users earn XP, level up, and unlock a leaderboard. Each found word shows a fun fact about that term.

Gamified · Education
03

Community

A social feed where users share posts, give likes, and support each other. Users can create posts, view friends' cycle phases (with consent), and find others at similar stages. A forecast view shows cycle sync with friends.

Social · Support
04

Settings & Personalization

Full profile management, notification preferences (daily/weekly/monthly), cycle preferences, health condition selection (endometriosis, PCOS, PMDD, anxiety, depression), and a shop with purchasable app themes.

Personalization · Accessibility

Sync & Seek Deep Dive

Education disguised as a game

The word-search game was one of Flowkeeper's most distinctive design decisions embedding reproductive health vocabulary into play, so learning feels like a reward rather than a lesson.

01

Welcome & level system

Users see their current level and progress bar before starting. Winning games earns XP and unlocks higher levels turning a health app into something with replay value.

02

10 cycle words to find

Estrogen, Luteal, Follicular, Menstrual, Period, Comfort, Wellness, Ovulation, Phase, and Cycle each hidden in a 10×10 grid. As each word is found, a fun fact overlay appears.

03

Progressive word states

9 interaction states were designed one per word found. Trigger dots appear on the grid as hints. This required careful prototyping to simulate real search-and-find gameplay.

04

Fun fact overlays

After each word is found, a card pops up with an interesting fact e.g. "While often called a 'female' hormone, estrogen is produced in both men and women." Learning through delight.

05

Congrats & level up

A celebratory screen appears on game completion with a flower wand illustration, XP earned, and options to share, play again, or view the leaderboard.

06

Leaderboard

A social leaderboard shows top players by name and level, encouraging friendly competition. Empty states invite users to bring friends into the app a built-in growth mechanic.

Reflections

What we learned building Flowkeeper

This project pushed us to design for a deeply personal subject with care, nuance, and creativity.

01

Health design needs warmth

Clinical UI patterns feel cold in a reproductive health context. Every design decision the pink palette, the script wordmark, the friendly tone was intentional in making users feel safe and welcome.

02

Gamification teaches without preaching

Sync & Seek proved that embedding education into gameplay is more effective than a "Learn" tab. Users seek out the words, find the fact, and move on retaining knowledge without noticing they're learning.

03

Community requires intentional safety

The Community tab required careful thinking about privacy users sharing cycle phases with friends is powerful, but must be fully opt-in. Consent-first design is especially critical in health apps.

04

One screen, many journeys

The "Your Journey" settings (Cycle, Conceive, Partner) taught us that the same app must work for radically different users. Designing flexible systems not just fixed screens was the real challenge.

05

Streaks drive retention

Adding the flame streak counter to the cycle view was a late decision that had outsized impact. Habit-forming mechanics like streaks are powerful motivators for daily health tracking behaviors.

06

Scope creep is a design signal

The file grew to 30+ screens across a single page. That sprawl was a sign the project had real depth and a reminder that organization is a design skill just as much as visual craft.
